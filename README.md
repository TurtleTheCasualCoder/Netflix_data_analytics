# Netflix Data Analytics

## Overview
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on Netflix dataset to uncover insights on content distribution, genres, ratings, and country-wise trends.

## Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Power BI
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Analysis
- Content distribution by country (Top 10 + Others)
- Most frequent genres and ratings
- Year-wise content growth
- Data cleaning & preprocessing

## Questions
# Q1. How has Netflix’s content library grown over the years?
Netflix’s content additions increased steadily and peaked around 2019–2020, followed by a decline, indicating a shift in content expansion strategy.

# Q2. Does Netflix focus more on Movies or TV Shows?
Movies dominate the platform, making up approximately 69% of the total content, while TV Shows account for the remaining share.

# Q3. Which content ratings are most frequent on Netflix?
TV-MA and TV-14 are the most common ratings, suggesting Netflix primarily targets mature and young-adult audiences.

# Q4. What genres are most common on Netflix?
Documentaries, Stand-Up Comedy, and International Dramas are among the most frequent genres, reflecting Netflix’s diverse content strategy.

# Q5. Which countries contribute the most content to Netflix?
The United States leads content production, followed by India and the United Kingdom, highlighting Netflix’s global and regional focus.

## Future Outlook Based on Analytics
1.Netflix is expected to focus more on TV Shows and international content rather than rapid expansion of its overall library.

2.Future content additions are likely to remain stable or selective rather than return to the rapid expansion seen around 2020.

3.TV Shows are expected to gain a larger share of Netflix’s catalog due to their higher potential for long-term viewer engagement.

4.Netflix is likely to continue prioritizing TV-MA and TV-14 content to cater to its core young-adult and mature audience.

5.Documentaries and stand-up comedy are expected to remain key genres due to their lower production cost and consistent demand.
